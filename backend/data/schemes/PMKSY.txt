SCHEME: Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY)
TYPE: Irrigation & Water Management
MINISTRY: Ministry of Jal Shakti / Ministry of Agriculture
LAUNCH YEAR: 2015

DESCRIPTION:
PMKSY aims to achieve "Har Khet Ko Pani" (water to every field) and "More Crop Per Drop" through convergence of irrigation investments, increase in cultivated area under irrigation, improve on-farm water use efficiency, introduce sustainable water conservation practices, and create water sources at micro level. The scheme promotes micro-irrigation (drip and sprinkler systems).

BENEFITS:
- Subsidy on drip irrigation systems: up to 55% for general farmers, 65% for SC/ST and small/marginal farmers
- Subsidy on sprinkler irrigation systems: up to 50% for general, 60% for small/marginal farmers
- Per drop more crop: micro-irrigation conserves water and improves yields
- Reduces humidity stress on crops (high humidity causes fungal diseases)
- Watershed development funding for drought-prone areas
- AIBP (Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Programme) funding for incomplete major/medium projects

ELIGIBILITY:
- All categories of farmers are eligible
- Priority given to small and marginal farmers (land up to 2 hectares)
- SC/ST farmers get higher subsidy rates
- Farmers in drought-prone or water-scarce districts get priority

APPLICATION PROCESS:
1. Register on state agriculture department portal or visit local agriculture office
2. Submit land records, Aadhaar, bank account details
3. Get soil testing done for suitable micro-irrigation system recommendation
4. Install drip/sprinkler system through empanelled vendors
5. Claim subsidy post-installation inspection by district agriculture officer

BENEFIT FOR DISEASE MANAGEMENT:
- Proper irrigation reduces humidity around leaves → fewer fungal diseases
- Drip irrigation avoids wetting leaves → prevents Brown Spot, Leaf Blast, Blight
- Water stress reduction improves crop immunity and recovery from disease

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Farmers in areas with water scarcity or irregular rainfall
- Farmers growing rain-sensitive crops (rice, wheat, vegetables)
- Farmers experiencing weather-related crop disease from humidity

KEYWORDS: irrigation, drip, sprinkler, water, humidity, rainfall, drought, moisture, water scarcity, crop protection

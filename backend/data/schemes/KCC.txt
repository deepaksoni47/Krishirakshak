SCHEME: Kisan Credit Card (KCC)
TYPE: Agricultural Credit / Financial Support
MINISTRY: Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare / RBI / NABARD
LAUNCH YEAR: 1998

DESCRIPTION:
The Kisan Credit Card (KCC) scheme provides farmers with affordable, timely, and adequate credit support for their agricultural and allied activities. It is a revolving credit facility with a flexible repayment schedule based on harvesting and marketing period. In 2020, the scheme was extended to fishermen and animal husbandry farmers as well.

BENEFITS:
- Credit limit based on land holding, crop pattern, and scale of finance
- Flexible repayment: repay after harvest season without penalty
- Single credit facility for all farm needs: inputs, maintenance, post-harvest, consumption
- Interest subvention: government subsidizes interest to reduce rates to 4% per annum
- Crop insurance coverage included automatically under PMFBY
- Personal accident insurance up to INR 50,000 for accidental death/permanent disability
- Coverage up to INR 3 lakh at 4% p.a. for short-term crop loans
- ATM-enabled card for easy fund access

ELIGIBILITY:
- All individual or joint borrower farmers who are owner-cultivators
- Tenant farmers, oral lessees, and sharecroppers
- Self Help Groups (SHGs) or Joint Liability Groups (JLGs) of farmers
- No minimum land ownership requirement for animal husbandry and fisheries

CREDIT LIMIT:
- Determined by Crop Cultivation Expenses + Post-harvest expenses + Maintenance of farm assets
- Enhanced limit for allied activities like animal husbandry and fisheries
- Revolving credit: once repaid, full limit available again next season

APPLICATION PROCESS:
1. Visit nearest bank branch (nationalized, cooperative, or regional rural bank)
2. Submit application with Aadhaar, land records, passport photo, and bank account details
3. Bank assessment of credit requirement
4. KCC issued within 14 working days of complete application submission
5. Card valid for 5 years, renewable annually

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Farmers needing credit to purchase seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, fungicides
- Farmers with medium to high treatment costs (INR 300–800+)
- Farmers requiring post-harvest credit

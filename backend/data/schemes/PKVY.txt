SCHEME: Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)
TYPE: Organic Farming Support
MINISTRY: Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare
LAUNCH YEAR: 2015

DESCRIPTION:
Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) promotes organic farming in India through cluster-based approach. It helps farmers adopt sustainable agricultural practices, reduce chemical input dependency, and improve soil health. The scheme provides financial assistance for organic certification, organic inputs, and market linkage.

BENEFITS:
- Financial assistance of INR 50,000 per hectare for 3 years (INR 31,000 for organic inputs)
- INR 8,800 per hectare for organic certification
- Training and capacity building for farmers
- Market linkage support and organic produce branding
- Improved soil health through organic practices
- Organic inputs like compost, vermicompost, bio-pesticides supported
- Bio-pesticides and biopesticide units supported for disease management

ELIGIBILITY:
- Farmers willing to adopt organic farming for minimum 3 years
- Must form clusters of minimum 50 acres with 20 or more farmers
- Landholding farmers with valid land records
- Preference to small and marginal farmers

APPLICATION PROCESS:
1. Form a cluster of at least 20 farmers covering 50 acres
2. Apply through State Agriculture Department
3. Elect cluster in-charge and form documentation
4. Undergo organic farming training
5. Begin conversion period (3 years from chemical to organic)

DISEASE MANAGEMENT CONNECTION:
- Organic farming builds soil microbiome that suppresses soil-borne diseases
- Bio-pesticides and bio-fungicides supported under PKVY
- Reduces chemical fungicide dependency over time
- Vermicompost improves plant immunity against fungal diseases

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Farmers with repeated chemical-resistant disease outbreaks
- Farmers concerned about chemical residues
- Farmers wanting to reduce long-term treatment costs

KEYWORDS: organic farming, bio-pesticide, vermicompost, compost, soil health, natural farming, bio-fungicide, sustainable agriculture, chemical free

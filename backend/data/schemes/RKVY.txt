SCHEME: Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY)
TYPE: Agricultural Infrastructure & Development
MINISTRY: Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare
LAUNCH YEAR: 2007 (Restructured 2017 as RKVY-RAFTAAR)

DESCRIPTION:
RKVY (Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana) is a centrally sponsored scheme that strengthens farmers' efforts to maximize agricultural production and productivity by supporting state governments in developing comprehensive plans for agriculture and allied sectors. RKVY-RAFTAAR focuses on innovation, agri-entrepreneurship, and value chain development.

BENEFITS:
- Funding for setting up farm machinery banks for custom hiring
- Support for post-harvest storage infrastructure
- Setting up soil testing labs and mobile soil testing units
- Support for crop protection measures and Integrated Pest Management (IPM) centres
- Grants for setting up demonstration plots of resistant crop varieties
- Agricultural extension and farmer training programs
- Drone technology support for precision agriculture and pesticide spraying
- Cold storage and food processing infrastructure support

ELIGIBILITY:
- Individual farmers, farmer groups, FPOs (Farmer Producer Organizations)
- Agricultural entrepreneurs and agri-startups
- State agricultural universities and research institutions
- Self-help groups engaged in agriculture

APPLICATION PROCESS:
1. Apply through State Department of Agriculture
2. Project proposals submitted to State Level Sanctioning Committee (SLSC)
3. SLSC reviews and forwards eligible proposals for funding
4. Funding released in instalments upon meeting milestones

AGRI-STARTUPS:
- Agri-entrepreneurs can apply for grants up to INR 25 lakh under RKVY-RAFTAAR
- Incubation support available through agricultural universities

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Farmers wanting precision spraying drones for disease management
- Farmers groups seeking farm machinery for crop protection
- Farmers needing training on disease-resistant varieties

KEYWORDS: agricultural development, farm machinery, infrastructure, crop protection, integrated pest management, disease resistant varieties, drone spraying, extension

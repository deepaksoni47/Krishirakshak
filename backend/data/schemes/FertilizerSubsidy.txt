SCHEME: Fertilizer Subsidy Scheme
TYPE: Input Subsidy
MINISTRY: Ministry of Chemicals & Fertilizers
LAUNCH YEAR: Ongoing (DBT reforms from 2016)

DESCRIPTION:
The Government of India provides fertilizer subsidies to ensure affordable access to fertilizers like urea, DAP (Di-Ammonium Phosphate), MOP (Muriate of Potash), and complex fertilizers. Subsidies are provided through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) and point-of-sale machines at retail outlets. Properly balanced fertilization is critical for disease prevention.

BENEFITS:
- Urea: maximum retail price fixed at INR 242 per 45 kg bag (heavily subsidized from actual cost of INR 1,400+)
- DAP: subsidized at INR 1,350 per bag (actual cost INR 4,000+)
- MOP: subsidized price for potassium fertilizer
- Nutrient-Based Subsidy (NBS) for P and K fertilizers
- DBT through PoS machine at fertilizer retailer using Aadhaar authentication
- Micro-nutrient supplements (zinc, boron, iron) subsidized under NBS

ELIGIBILITY:
- All registered farmers are eligible
- Purchase recorded through Aadhaar-authenticated PoS system
- No minimum or maximum purchase limit for individual farmers

HOW TO ACCESS:
1. Visit registered fertilizer dealer/retailer
2. Authenticate using Aadhaar biometric or OTP at PoS machine
3. Purchase fertilizer at subsidized MRP
4. Transaction logged in Meri Fasal Mera Byora or DBT portal

DISEASE PREVENTION CONNECTION:
- Balanced NPK prevents nutrient deficiency diseases (yellowing, tip burn, scorching)
- Excess nitrogen increases susceptibility to fungal diseases like blast and blight
- Potassium (K) improves plant immunity and disease resistance
- Soil Health Card + Fertilizer Subsidy together reduce disease risk

BEST SUITED FOR:
- Farmers facing nutrient deficiency symptoms alongside disease
- Farmers spending high amounts on commercial fertilizers
- Farmers in areas with repeated crop health issues
